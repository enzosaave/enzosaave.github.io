Biography

From Argentina to the Canary Islands

My journey in astrophysics began in Argentina with the GARX group led by Jorge Combi, where I studied high-mass X-ray binaries. This experience ignited my fascination with compact objects and their enigmatic behavior.

Now based in Tenerife, Spain, I am pursuing my doctorate at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC) under the supervision of Montserrat Armas Padilla and Teo Muñoz Darias. The primary objective of my thesis is to investigate accretion phenomena in low-mass X-ray binaries, including ultracompact X-ray binaries—a subtype characterized by orbital periods shorter than 80 minutes.

To achieve this, I employ a comprehensive multi-wavelength approach, combining infrared, optical, and X-ray observations to reveal the hidden physics of these systems during their less understood quiescent phases.
