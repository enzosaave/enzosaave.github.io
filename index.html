About Me

My journey in astrophysics began in Argentina with the GARX group led by Jorge Combi, where I studied high-mass X-ray binaries. This experience ignited my fascination with compact objects and their enigmatic behaviour.


Now based in Tenerife, Spain, I am pursuing my doctorate at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC) under the supervision of Montserrat Armas Padilla and Teo Muñoz Darias. My research employs a distinctive multi-wavelength approach—combining X-ray, optical, and infrared observations—to reveal the hidden physics of low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) during their less understood quiescent phases.
