About Me

My journey in astrophysics began in Argentina with the GARX group led by Jorge Combi, where I studied high-mass X-ray binaries. This experience ignited my fascination with compact objects and their enigmatic behaviour.

Now based in Tenerife, Spain, I am pursuing my doctorate at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC) under the supervision of Montserrat Armas Padilla and Teo Muñoz Darias. My focus lies on understanding accretion physics in the most extreme environments in the universe.

Research Interests

My research employs a distinctive multi-wavelength approach—combining X-ray, optical, and infrared observations—to reveal the hidden physics of low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs).

I specifically investigate accretion phenomena in low-luminosity states, with a special interest in ultracompact X-ray binaries. These rare systems, composed of a neutron star and a degenerate companion, exhibit orbital periods shorter than 80 minutes, offering a unique laboratory for testing accretion theory.
